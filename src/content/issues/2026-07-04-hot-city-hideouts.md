**Weekend of July 4–5.** Jakarta looks warm, humid, and a bit cloud-mooded, so you two should keep the plan cute but flexible. Pick one indoor anchor, one snacky wander, and double-check official pages before leaving because lineups, ticket slots, and weather can shift fast.

## 1. Jakarta Fair Kemayoran
**Date:** 4–5 July 2026; check daily lineup
**Area:** Central — JIExpo Kemayoran
**Why go:** The big Jakarta birthday-season fair is still the easiest “we do not need a perfect plan” date: food, shopping, bright booths, little games, and concert chaos if you want extra noise. Go for snacks first, then let the fair decide the rest.
**Rough budget:** Paid entry + snacks/shopping
**Weather:** Good for a cloudy afternoon or evening; expect some outdoor walking between halls.
**Source:** [Jakarta Fair official site](https://www.jakartafair.co.id/)

## 2. Museum MACAN exhibitions
**Date:** 13 June–4 October 2026; on this weekend
**Area:** West — AKR Tower, Kebon Jeruk
**Why go:** A clean indoor reset when the city feels sticky. One ticket currently covers several exhibitions and special presentations, so it is a good low-drama plan when you two want to look at things, whisper opinions, and avoid the sun.
**Rough budget:** Museum ticket; promo may apply
**Weather:** Best heat-and-rain shelter pick; book/check ticket slots before going.
**Source:** [Museum MACAN tickets](https://www.museummacan.org/tickets)

## 3. Ancol seaside loop
**Date:** Available 4–5 July 2026; check same-day events
**Area:** North — Ancol / Pasar Seni / beach area
**Why go:** Keep it breezy and unserious: beach walk, Pasar Seni, maybe Sea World or Bird Land if the weather turns weird. Do not over-pack the route; the sweet spot is one attraction, one snack, one slow sunset attempt.
**Rough budget:** Rp 35k+ entry; attractions extra
**Weather:** Go morning or late afternoon; move indoors if rain clouds stack up.
**Source:** [Ancol official site](https://www.ancol.com/)

## 4. Ragunan green morning
**Date:** Available 4–5 July 2026; Tuesday–Sunday
**Area:** South — Taman Margasatwa Ragunan, Pasar Minggu
**Why go:** A soft, leafy morning before Jakarta gets loud. Walk a little, visit just a few zones, grab simple snacks, then leave while it still feels like a cute date and not a survival trek.
**Rough budget:** Low entry + transport/snacks
**Weather:** Morning only; bring water and do not force it if the sky looks dramatic.
**Source:** [Ragunan official site](https://ragunanzoo.jakarta.go.id/language/en/)

## If you only do three things
1. **Jakarta Fair Kemayoran** — the most weekend-coded, snack-heavy anchor.
2. **Museum MACAN exhibitions** — the safest cool-down if it gets hot or rainy.
3. **Ancol seaside loop** — the best light outdoor plan if the sky behaves.
