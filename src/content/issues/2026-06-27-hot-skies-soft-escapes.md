**Weekend of June 27–28.** It looks like a hot Jakarta weekend with possible afternoon storms, so you two should bias toward morning starts, indoor venues, or plans that are easy to abandon gracefully. The vibe is less “pack the day” and more “choose one anchor, then keep a raincheck nearby.”

## 1. AirAsia HYROX Jakarta
**Date:** 27–28 June 2026
**Area:** North — NICE PIK 2
**Why go:** A big indoor fitness-race weekend with running, workout stations, solo, doubles, and relay formats. Worth it if you two want something energetic to watch, or if you want that “maybe we should train again” kind of spark without betting the date on dry weather.
**Rough budget:** Spectator ticket varies
**Weather:** Indoor plan, so it is the safest pick if the afternoon turns stormy.
**Source:** [HYROX official event page](https://hyrox.com/event/hyrox-jakarta/)

## 2. Jakarta Fair Kemayoran
**Date:** 11 June–12 July 2026; available this weekend
**Area:** Central — JIExpo Kemayoran
**Why go:** The classic Jakarta birthday-season fair: snacks, shopping, music, bright booths, and a crowd that feels very Jakarta. It is worth going if you two want a lively, low-pressure evening where the plan can simply be “walk, eat, look around, go home when tired.”
**Rough budget:** Paid entry + food/shopping
**Weather:** Go later in the day after the worst heat, but expect some outdoor movement between halls.
**Source:** [Jakarta Fair official site](https://www.jakartafair.co.id/)

## 3. Ancol seaside loop
**Date:** Available this weekend; check same-day promos/events
**Area:** North — Ancol / Pasar Seni / beach area
**Why go:** A flexible north-side plan for sunset, Pasar Seni, casual food, or one paid attraction if you two want more structure. It is best as a light “see how we feel” plan, not a packed itinerary.
**Rough budget:** Rp 35k+ entry, more for attractions
**Weather:** Aim for early morning or late afternoon; skip the beachy parts if rain builds.
**Source:** [Ancol official site](https://www.ancol.com/)

## 4. Ragunan green morning
**Date:** Available this weekend; Tuesday–Sunday opening pattern
**Area:** South — Taman Margasatwa Ragunan
**Why go:** A calmer, leafy morning option before Jakarta gets too hot. You two can keep it simple: walk, see a few zones, grab a snack, and leave before the afternoon weather gets messy.
**Rough budget:** Low entry + transport/snacks
**Weather:** Make it a morning plan; avoid turning it into a long outdoor day.
**Source:** [Ragunan official site](https://ragunanzoo.jakarta.go.id/language/en/)

## 5. Kota Tua museum crawl
**Date:** Available this weekend; check museum hours before going
**Area:** Old Town — Taman Fatahillah / Museum Sejarah Jakarta
**Why go:** A softer heritage plan: walk Taman Fatahillah before it gets too hot, step into Museum Sejarah Jakarta or nearby galleries, then sit down for coffee or lunch. Worth it if you two want a conversation-friendly date instead of another mall-only weekend.
**Rough budget:** Rp 25–150k each
**Weather:** Morning is the move; keep this short if clouds build after lunch.
**Source:** [Jakarta History Museum overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jakarta_History_Museum)

## 6. ASHTA District 8 raincheck
**Date:** Available this weekend; use as flexible fallback
**Area:** South — ASHTA District 8, SCBD
**Why go:** Keep this as the clean backup when rain, traffic, or decision fatigue hits: coffee, browsing, dinner, and an easy exit. It is not the most “eventy” pick, but it gives you two a comfortable place to land if outdoor plans feel too much.
**Rough budget:** Rp 75–250k each
**Weather:** Fully indoor-friendly; best used as your afternoon raincheck.
**Source:** [District 8 / ASHTA overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/District_8_Jakarta)

## My top 3
1. **HYROX** — most rain-safe and energetic.
2. **Jakarta Fair** — best all-in-one Jakarta weekend vibe.
3. **Kota Tua** — best slow morning together before the heat and rain risk.
